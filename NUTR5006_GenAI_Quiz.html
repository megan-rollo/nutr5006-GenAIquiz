Responsible Use of Generative AI in Research

This self-assessment quiz is designed to help you check your understanding of the responsible and ethical use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) tools in research before you begin your NUTR5006 project.

The quiz covers the key principles set out in the NUTR5006 Guidelines for the Appropriate Use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (2026), including when and how GenAI tools may be used across the research process, what is never appropriate, and your obligations around disclosure, record-keeping, privacy, and copyright. It also draws on the Curtin University HDR GenAI Guidelines and the NHMRC Guide for Assessing Research Involving AI, both available on Blackboard.

There are 17 questions covering five areas: literature discovery and synthesis, data analysis and interpretation, manuscript writing, responsible GenAI use, and supervisor approval and record-keeping. Each question includes feedback explaining the correct answer and the reasoning behind it.

Before attempting the quiz, you are encouraged to read the NUTR5006 GenAI guidelines in full. The quiz is not assessed — it is a tool to help you identify any areas where you may benefit from reviewing the guidance before committing to your approach.

Additional resources (available on Blackboard)

  • NHMRC Guide for Assessing Research Involving AI (2025)
  • Committee on Publication Ethics — Authorship and AI Tools (2024)
  • Ng, J.Y.Y. (2026). A structured framework for GenAI prompt engineering for health and medical researchers. Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence
